Exercise: Obstacle Course Walking

Instructions

1. Set up an obstacle course in a safe, open area with items like cones, small hurdles, or even cushions placed on the ground.

2. Start with simple obstacles placed at varying distances apart.

3. Guide the individual to walk through the course, emphasizing proper gait mechanics such as heel-to-toe movement, swinging of arms, and maintaining balance.

4. Encourage the individual to step over or around obstacles, adapting their stride and pace as needed.

5. Gradually increase the complexity of the course by adding more obstacles or arranging them in challenging patterns.

6. Provide verbal cues and feedback on posture, balance, and coordination throughout the exercise.

7. Aim for multiple repetitions of walking through the course, gradually improving speed and confidence.

8. Modify the course based on the individual's progress and abilities, always prioritizing safety and proper form.

Exercise: One Leg Balance

Instructions

1. Begin by standing tall with your feet hip-width apart and your arms relaxed at your sides.

2. Shift your weight slightly onto one foot while keeping the other foot lightly touching the ground for support.

3. Slowly lift the foot off the ground, bringing the knee up towards hip level.

4. Find a focal point in front of you to help maintain balance and focus your gaze on it.

5. Engage your core muscles to help stabilize your body.

6. Hold this position for as long as you can maintain your balance, aiming for at least 10-20 seconds.

7.If you lose your balance, gently place the lifted foot back on the ground and try again.

8. Repeat the exercise on the opposite leg, focusing on maintaining good posture and stability throughout.

9. Gradually increase the duration of each hold as you become more comfortable with the exercise.

10. Aim for 2-3 sets of 3-5 repetitions on each leg, gradually increasing the duration of each hold as your balance improves.

Exercise: Working Memory Game

Instructions

1. Gather a set of matching pairs of cards or objects (e.g., playing cards, picture cards, or even small household items).

2. Shuffle the cards and lay them face down in a grid pattern on a table or flat surface.

3. The player begins by flipping over two cards, trying to find a matching pair.

4. If the cards match, the player keeps them and gets another turn.

5. If the cards do not match, they are flipped face down again, and it's the next player's turn.

6. Players take turns flipping over pairs of cards until all matches have been found.

7. The player with the most matches at the end of the game wins.

Exercise: Forward Jump

Instructions

1. Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart and your arms relaxed at your sides.

2. Bend your knees slightly and engage your core muscles to prepare for the jump.

3. Swing your arms backward to generate momentum.

4. Push off the balls of your feet and propel yourself forward with both legs.

5. As you jump, swing your arms forward to help lift your body off the ground.

6. Aim to jump forward as far as you can while maintaining control and balance.

7. Land softly on the balls of your feet, bending your knees to absorb the impact.

8. Keep your chest up and your gaze forward throughout the jump.

9. Practice landing with your weight evenly distributed between both feet.

10. Repeat the forward jump, focusing on increasing your distance with each attempt.
